# StrategicAI Narrative Constitution

## Purpose

This document governs how StrategicAI explains why it exists, what it does, what a customer can expect, and what the public site is allowed to claim.

It is a narrative control, not a description of every future capability. Product architecture, engagement delivery, pilot work, and planned capabilities may inform the story only at the level their evidence supports.

No public copy may be implemented from this package until this document and its companion copy deck are marked `APPROVED`.

## Locked Narrative Hierarchy

- **Company thesis:** Organizational change fails when reality, decisions, authority, work, and outcomes become disconnected.
- **StrategicAI's role:** Create a governed path through consequential organizational change.
- **Commercial promise:** Change with clarity, control, and proof.
- **Commercial outcome:** Confident, governed change.
- **Enabling organizational state:** Coherence.
- **Entry artifact:** Executive Brief.
- **Product progression:** Uncertainty -> shared reality -> governed intervention -> coordinated execution -> proof -> institutional memory.

These ideas are related but not interchangeable. The company thesis explains why StrategicAI must exist. The role explains what StrategicAI contributes. The promise explains what the customer can expect to gain. Coherence is the organizational state that makes governed change possible. The Executive Brief is the safest first step, not the complete identity of the company.

## Primary Audience

StrategicAI is for leaders who must move an organization forward without gambling its stability, people, or credibility.

The primary reader is the person who remains accountable for the organizational result: a founder, CEO, owner, executive director, city manager, or operating executive. The site should address that person's uncertainty, authority, duty of care, and need to move safely.

Functional operators, AI leaders, consultants, and transformation teams may be participants or secondary buyers. The core narrative must not be reduced to a company-size segment, growth-stage persona, or transformation job title.

## The Buyer Tension

Organizations often know that change is necessary while depending on the people, processes, systems, and relationships that change may disrupt. AI increases both the opportunity and the uncertainty.

The buyer is asking:

- What should change first?
- What might break?
- Who has the authority to decide?
- How will people participate without being flattened or surveilled?
- Are we changing the right process?
- How will we know whether the intervention worked?
- Will this become another abandoned initiative?

StrategicAI should not exploit this fear. It should demonstrate a more governable path.

## StrategicAI's Mechanism

StrategicAI begins by forming a structured operating picture from organization context, leadership intent, and team evidence. That picture creates a common reference without pretending that every perspective is identical or automatically true.

The operating picture supports a staged commercial and product progression:

1. **Executive Brief - Mirror:** Reflects visible pain, friction, strain, and operating symptoms. It does not diagnose root cause or prescribe an intervention.
2. **Diagnostic - Explanation:** Identifies the governing constraint and explains why the visible symptoms cohere. It is a paid engagement after the Executive Brief.
3. **Roadmap - Intervention architecture:** Establishes sequence, priorities, ownership implications, milestones, and the first execution structure. It does not imply that implementation is complete.
4. **Implementation and execution surfaces - Operational construction:** Builds the governed connectors, workflows, and surfaces required by the approved intervention. Availability and scope depend on the engagement.
5. **Continuity - Evidence and learning:** Where implemented and supported, outcomes, receipts, and updated operating state can preserve what happened and inform what follows.

The complete progression describes StrategicAI's architecture and direction. It must not be presented as universally automated, continuously active, self-service, or included in every engagement.

## AI Framing

AI is central to StrategicAI's market timing, intelligence, and product identity. It should appear in the homepage supporting narrative and product story, but it must not own the main headline or narrow the company to AI transformation.

Approved framing:

- AI is accelerating what organizations can change and increasing the cost of changing the wrong thing.
- StrategicAI helps leaders determine where AI belongs, introduce it under clear authority, and evaluate the organizational result.
- AI supports synthesis, interpretation, and bounded assistance within the authority and commercial limits of each stage.
- StrategicAI governs organizational change, including but not limited to AI adoption.

Unapproved framing:

- AI autonomously discovers organizational truth.
- AI decides what the organization should do.
- AI transformation is the only reason to use StrategicAI.
- Automation itself is the customer outcome.

## Meaning of Clarity, Control, and Proof

### Clarity

A credible, shared view of what is happening, grounded in organization context, leadership intent, and team evidence. Clarity does not mean perfect information, consensus, or an automatic diagnosis.

### Control

Visible authority, deliberate intervention boundaries, explicit ownership, and a staged path through change. Control does not mean centralized executive domination, employee surveillance, or removing human judgment.

### Proof

Evidence appropriate to the stage: completed intake, an immutable artifact, an approved decision, an execution receipt, or an observed outcome. Proof does not mean guaranteed success, universal causal certainty, or continuous verification where no such runtime has been implemented.

## Claim Maturity and Evidence

Every public product claim must be traceable to an existing product capability, engagement deliverable, or explicitly bounded pilot capability. The narrative may describe the full StrategicAI progression, but it must not imply that every stage is currently self-service, automated, continuously active, or universally included.

Claims involving routed execution, operational receipts, outcome verification, persistent monitoring, institutional memory, or AI-directed intervention must state or preserve the correct delivery boundary.

### Maturity Classes

| Class | Meaning | Public-copy rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Available now | A customer-accessible capability that has been successfully exercised end to end in the current production environment under its stated conditions. | May be stated directly without implying universal completion or availability beyond the verified conditions. |
| Delivered through a StrategicAI engagement | A capability the current delivery team can repeatably provide under a defined commercial scope, with a verified delivery path - not merely an architectural possibility. | Must identify or preserve the engagement boundary. |
| Pilot capability | A bounded capability that has passed its stated pilot acceptance path for a specific tenant, environment, or use case. Pilot readiness does not imply generalized availability. | Must use conditional or pilot-bounded language; it cannot represent the default customer experience. |
| Planned capability | The architecture or commercial model anticipates the capability, but generalized delivery has not earned current-product status. | May appear only as future direction when clearly labeled; omit from core conversion copy. |
| Prohibited until evidenced | The claim is not supported, is inherently misleading, or exceeds StrategicAI's authority. | Must not appear publicly. |

Source code, a rendered surface, or an isolated service path is not sufficient by itself. Each `Available now` claim requires a current verification receipt or documented production acceptance path.

Until that evidence is attached, the claim must be classified as `Pending Available-now verification` and cannot authorize public copy for release.

## Commercial Ladder and Boundaries

The public story must preserve this order:

1. **Executive Brief:** Free Mirror generated through platform onboarding and team input.
2. **Diagnostic:** Paid explanatory engagement after the Executive Brief and Discovery Call boundary.
3. **Roadmap:** Paid intervention sequence and decision structure.
4. **Implementation / Execution:** Scoped construction of operational systems and surfaces.
5. **Ongoing continuity:** Available only where contracted and implemented.

Each stage must earn the next. No stage may borrow the value or authority of a later stage to make itself sound more complete.

## Forbidden Positioning

StrategicAI must not be presented as:

- An AI automation agency.
- A generic strategy consultancy.
- A diagnostic or assessment product.
- A roadmap generator.
- A project-management platform.
- A replacement for accountable leadership.
- A system that autonomously decides what an organization should do.
- A guarantee that organizational change will succeed.
- A product exclusively for AI transformation.
- A product exclusively for SMBs, startups, or enterprise transformation offices.

The following language and conceptual errors are prohibited:

- "You do not have an execution problem."
- "AI will transform your business."
- "We tell you what is wrong."
- "One platform runs your entire organization."
- Claims of automatic organizational truth.
- Claims that every engagement includes automated routing, monitoring, receipts, or outcome verification.
- Language implying employee surveillance.
- Language equating control with centralized executive domination.
- Language implying that StrategicAI replaces leadership authority or human judgment.
- Language promising certainty where the product provides evidence, interpretation, or a governed decision process.

### Use Carefully

- **Digital Twin:** Introduce only after explaining the structured operating model; never lead with the label.
- **Proof:** Name the evidence type or preserve the engagement/pilot boundary.
- **Control:** Pair with authority, participation, and visibility; never imply domination.
- **AI agents / automation:** Use only for a concrete, evidenced capability and stated scope.
- **Institutional memory:** Use as an architectural outcome or bounded implementation result, not a universal current feature.
- **Mission Control:** State that availability and data depth depend on implementation scope.
